\section{CQ1 -- NON DELETE Requests to coap.me}

\subsection{CQ1a}

\textbf{Filter:} All NON-Confirmable (type=1) DELETE (code=4) requests directed to
\texttt{coap.me} (IP \texttt{134.102.218.18}, resolved via DNS in the capture),
matched with their responses by token.

Out of 20 NON DELETE requests to \texttt{coap.me}, only \textbf{1} received a
successful (2.xx) response:

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
MID & Resource & Response Code & Meaning \\
\midrule
30800 & /validate & 2.02 & Deleted \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{NON DELETE requests with successful response}
\end{table}

The remaining 19 requests all received \texttt{4.05 Method Not Allowed}.

\textbf{Answer CQ1a:} 1 request. \textbf{MID = 30800}.

\subsection{CQ1b}

Although the server responded with \texttt{2.02 Deleted} to MID 30800, a subsequent
GET request to \texttt{/validate} (frame 11363) still received a \texttt{2.05 Content}
response (frame 11364), proving the resource was not actually deleted.
\texttt{coap.me} is a public test server that acknowledges the DELETE method on
\texttt{/validate} but does not truly remove the resource.

\textbf{Answer CQ1b: 0}

\section{CQ3 -- Observe Notifications for /dining\_room/temperature}

\subsection{CQ3a}

\textbf{Method:} Identified the observe registration (frame 4343: CON GET with
\texttt{Observe=0}, token \texttt{89b9}, to \texttt{127.0.0.1:5683}). Then collected all
responses from the server (srcport 5683) matching this token with the Observe option
present. Grouped by MID to eliminate retransmissions.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}cllcccc@{}}
\toprule
\# & MID & Type & Observe & Temp (\textdegree C) & ACKed & Retransmissions \\
\midrule
1  & 10588 & ACK & 1  & 19.97 & Yes & 0 \\
2  & 35    & CON & 1  & 22.32 & Yes & 0 \\
3  & 38    & CON & 2  & 18.85 & Yes & 0 \\
4  & 43    & CON & 3  & 25.98 & Yes & 0 \\
5  & 45    & CON & 4  & 15.59 & Yes & 0 \\
6  & 49    & CON & 5  & 25.35 & Yes & 0 \\
7  & 54    & CON & 6  & 26.21 & \textbf{No} & 7 \\
8  & 62    & CON & 7  & 27.53 & \textbf{No} & 3 \\
9  & 74    & CON & 8  & 26.98 & \textbf{No} & 1 \\
10 & 87    & CON & 9  & 25.32 & \textbf{No} & 1 \\
11 & 90    & CON & 10 & 19.57 & \textbf{No} & 1 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Unique observe notifications for \texttt{/dining\_room/temperature}}
\end{table}

\textbf{Answer CQ3a: 11}

\subsection{CQ3b}

Starting from notification \#7 (MID 54, Observe=6), the client stops acknowledging
CON notifications. MID~54 is retransmitted 7 times without receiving an ACK. According
to RFC~7641 \S4.5, when a CON notification fails (exhausts retransmissions without ACK),
the server should cancel the observe relationship. Despite this, the server continues
sending notifications \#8--11 (MIDs 62, 74, 87, 90), none of which are ever
acknowledged. All 5 unacknowledged notifications (MIDs 54, 62, 74, 87, 90) represent
wasted traffic.

\textbf{Answer CQ3b: 5}

\section{CQ4 -- MQTT-SN Messages from Local Broker (Port 1885)}

\textbf{Filter:} Configured tshark to decode UDP port 1885 as MQTT-SN
(\texttt{-d udp.port==1885,mqttsn}). Filtered for packets with
\texttt{udp.srcport == 1885} (messages FROM the broker to clients).

\textbf{Analysis:} 219 MQTT-SN messages were sent by clients \emph{to} port 1885
(\texttt{udp.dstport == 1885}). Every single one triggered an ICMP
``Destination Unreachable -- Port Unreachable'' (Type~3, Code~3) response, confirming
that no broker was listening on port 1885. Zero packets were observed with
\texttt{udp.srcport == 1885}, meaning the broker never sent any response.

\textbf{Answer CQ4: 0}
