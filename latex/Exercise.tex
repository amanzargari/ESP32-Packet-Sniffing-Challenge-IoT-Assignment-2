\section{EQ1a -- CoAP Direct Communication}

The sensor transmits every 2 minutes, yielding $N = 60/2 = 30$ measurements per hour.
With CoAP CON PUT, each measurement involves two messages between sensor and actuator
(ideal channel, no retransmissions):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llrl@{}}
\toprule
Device & Action & Size & Energy \\
\midrule
Sensor   & TX CON PUT & 70\,B & $70 \times 8 \times 50 = 28{,}000$\,nJ \\
Sensor   & RX PUT ACK & 15\,B & $15 \times 8 \times 58 = 6{,}960$\,nJ \\
Actuator & RX CON PUT & 70\,B & $70 \times 8 \times 58 = 32{,}480$\,nJ \\
Actuator & TX PUT ACK & 15\,B & $15 \times 8 \times 50 = 6{,}000$\,nJ \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{Total per measurement} & 73{,}440\,nJ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Over 1 hour:
\[
E_{\text{CoAP}} = 30 \times 73{,}440 = 2{,}203{,}200 \text{\,nJ}
\]

\textbf{EQ1a = 2203.20\,\boldmath$\mu$J}

\section{EQ1b -- MQTT via Gateway (QoS 1)}

Devices start disconnected. The sensor connects, publishes, and disconnects for
each measurement (sleeping between). The actuator connects once, subscribes,
receives all 30 messages, then disconnects.

\subsection*{Sensor (30 connect--publish--disconnect cycles)}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llrl@{}}
\toprule
Direction & Messages & Bytes & Energy \\
\midrule
TX & CONNECT + PUBLISH + DISCONNECT & $60+75+60=195$\,B & $195 \times 8 \times 50 = 78{,}000$\,nJ \\
RX & CONNACK + PUBACK & $50+50=100$\,B & $100 \times 8 \times 58 = 46{,}400$\,nJ \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{Per cycle} & 124{,}400\,nJ \\
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{30 cycles} & 3{,}732{,}000\,nJ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection*{Actuator (1 setup + 30 receives + 1 teardown)}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llrl@{}}
\toprule
Phase & Messages & Bytes & Energy \\
\midrule
Setup TX & CONNECT + SUBSCRIBE & $60+65=125$\,B & 50{,}000\,nJ \\
Setup RX & CONNACK + SUBACK & $50+55=105$\,B & 48{,}720\,nJ \\
Per msg RX & PUBLISH & 75\,B & 34{,}800\,nJ \\
Per msg TX & PUBACK & 50\,B & 20{,}000\,nJ \\
Teardown TX & DISCONNECT & 60\,B & 24{,}000\,nJ \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{@{}l}{Actuator total ($98{,}720 + 30 \times 54{,}800 + 24{,}000$)} & 1{,}766{,}720\,nJ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\[
E_{\text{MQTT}} = 3{,}732{,}000 + 1{,}766{,}720 = 5{,}498{,}720 \text{\,nJ}
\]

\textbf{EQ1b = 5498.72\,\boldmath$\mu$J}

\bigskip
MQTT consumes approximately 2.5$\times$ the energy of CoAP due to
the per-measurement connect/disconnect overhead on the sensor side.

\section{EQ2 -- Actuator Wakes Every 30 Minutes}

\textbf{Protocol choice: MQTT} with \texttt{retain=1}, \texttt{clean\_session=1}, QoS~1.

\textbf{Justification:} The mains-powered gateway buffers the latest sensor reading
via the MQTT retain flag. The actuator wakes only twice per hour, connects,
subscribes, immediately receives the single retained message, and disconnects.
In contrast, CoAP direct communication fails for 28 out of 30 messages because
the actuator is sleeping and cannot ACK; each failure triggers 3 retransmissions
(MAX\_TRANSMISSIONS=3, RTO=30\,s), wasting sensor energy with no data delivered.

The optimized metric is \textbf{reliable data delivery}: MQTT guarantees the actuator
always receives the most recent temperature when it wakes, while CoAP delivers
only $\sim$7\% of sensor readings.

\textbf{Form answer (300 chars):}
\begin{quote}
\small
MQTT with retain=1, clean\_session=1, QoS 1. Gateway (mains-powered) buffers the latest sensor value. Actuator wakes every 30 min, connects, subscribes, gets 1 retained msg, disconnects. CoAP fails 28/30 messages (sleeping actuator), wasting retransmission energy. Optimized metric: reliable delivery.
\end{quote}
